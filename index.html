The Challenge of Ultra-Low-Cost E-commerce

The massive influx of ultra-low-cost products from Chinese platforms like **Temu** and **Shein** presents a triple challenge: undercutting local businesses, increasing environmental waste, and complicating customs compliance. This section details how key economies are responding, followed by an analysis for Thailand.

Global Responses to Influx

USA: De Minimis Loophole Crackdown

Focuses on closing trade exemptions and increasing scrutiny for safety/security reasons.

Key Policy:

The US government is targeting the **De Minimis exemption** (which allowed shipments valued under $800 to enter duty-free without extensive customs inspection). Proposed rules would deny this exemption to products subject to U.S.-China tariffs, and require more detailed information to curb illicit or unsafe imports like precursor chemicals.

**Source:** Asia Financial: US to Curb Cheap China Parcels: Temu, Shein Face Big Hit

Europe (EU): Sustainability & Liability

Focuses on making producers financially responsible for waste and ensuring product compliance.

Key Policy:

  • **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR):** Mandating producers (including those outside the EU) to cover costs for collecting, sorting, and recycling textile waste.
  • **Ecodesign Regulation:** Introducing requirements for durability and recyclability, and banning the **destruction of unsold clothing**.
  • **Customs Reform:** Planning to eliminate the duty-free exemption for goods under â‚¬150.

**Source:** European Parliament: Fast fashion: EU laws for sustainable textile consumption & Fashion United: EU targets fast fashion e-commerce with new customs regime

ASEAN Neighbors: Direct Taxation

Focuses on implementing or increasing taxes/duties on low-value imports to protect domestic sellers.

Key Policy:

  • **Malaysia:** Imposed a **10% Sales Tax** on imported goods purchased online below MYR 500 (approx. $108 USD) starting 2024.
  • **Thailand:** Extended the standard **7% VAT** to cover purchases valued under THB 1,500 (approx. $42 USD). (Note: Thailand has already taken initial steps).
  • **Indonesia:** Is considering imposing high import duties (up to 200%) on specific categories like imported fabrics and cosmetics.

**Source:** KrASIA: Southeast Asia pushes back on cheap Chinese imports

Policy Adoption for Thailand

Thailand has already implemented the **7% VAT** on low-value goods, aligning with the ASEAN "Direct Taxation" model. To further protect local markets and the environment, Thailand can adopt strategies from the USA and EU.

1. Enhance Product Compliance & Safety Checks (The Security Focus)

**Adaptation from USA:** While closing the de minimis tax loophole is achieved via VAT, Thailand can strengthen the **Customs Inspection** aspect. The goal is to move beyond revenue collection and focus on **product safety, quality, and anti-counterfeit measures**.

By requiring e-commerce platforms to provide **detailed product tariff codes and safety certifications** for all low-value shipments, Thai customs can better identify and stop unsafe or illicit goods, protecting consumers and legitimate manufacturers.

2. Introduce Eco-Taxes and Extended Producer Responsibility (The Sustainability Focus)

**Adaptation from EU/France:** Implement an **Eco-Modulation Fee** or tax on ultra-fast fashion items, similar to France's proposal of up to â‚¬10 per item. The funds generated should be ring-fenced to support Thailand's nascent **recycling infrastructure** and local, sustainable fashion businesses.

This shifts the environmental cost of disposal onto the high-volume importers, making the local production of durable goods more competitive and supporting Thailandâ€™s long-term sustainability goals.

3. Apply Targeted Sector-Specific Import Duties (The Local Industry Focus)

**Adaptation from Indonesia:** Instead of applying high tariffs broadly, Thailand could conduct a strategic review of sectors facing the most severe displacement (e.g., local textiles, ceramics, or handicrafts).

Implement targeted, temporary **safeguard tariffs** or higher specific duties on specific HS codes within these sensitive categories when imported in bulk via e-commerce channels, providing a crucial buffer for local MSMEs to adapt and innovate.
